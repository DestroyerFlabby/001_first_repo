# Source Rights Checklist

Use this before posting any media-page clip publicly. Downloading a video does not mean we have the right to repost it.

## Safest Footage To Use

### Original Footage

Footage you filmed or created yourself. This is the safest option because you control the rights.

### Creator-Provided Footage

Footage sent by a client, creator, or partner with clear permission to edit and post. Keep written permission in email, DM, or a contract.

### Public Domain Footage

Footage that is confirmed to be in the public domain. Still save the source URL and proof of status.

### Creative Commons Footage With Attribution

Footage with a Creative Commons license that allows the intended use. Check whether commercial use, edits, and attribution are allowed.

### Licensed Stock Footage

Footage from stock libraries where you have an active license. Save the license details and source URL.

## What To Avoid

- Copyrighted documentary clips without permission.
- TV or news footage without permission.
- Movie, sports, or broadcast clips without permission.
- Random YouTube downloads without rights.
- Clips where the license is unclear.
- Footage that says "no reuse" or has restrictive terms.

## Rights Status Labels

Use these labels in the content calendar:

- `original`
- `creator_permission`
- `public_domain`
- `creative_commons_attribution`
- `licensed_stock`
- `needs_review`
- `do_not_use`

## Practical Rule

If the rights are unclear, mark the clip as `needs_review` and do not post it publicly yet. The safest business asset is content we can confidently reuse, transform, and show to clients.
